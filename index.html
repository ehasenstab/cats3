Ragdoll

Ragdoll cats are personable and cuddly kitties. The universal fondness for the ragdoll cat extends back to the mid-1960s. Ragdoll cats love cuddles and attention, and their coat is often a light color with a darker face. Ragdoll cats are very slow to grow in size so it takes about four years for them to be full size. If you're looking for a cuddle buddy, a ragdoll cat is the perfect option.

Exotic Shorthair

Exotic shorthair cats are coined the lazy person's Persian cat because exotic shorthairs share a resemblance to Persian cats, but have a shorter coat of fur. Exotic shorthairs are great cats for families with young children. Exotic shorthairs tend to have bright colored eyes with bicolor fur coats. Although these cats have a low energy level, exotic shorthairs are highly affectionate and make great pets to pair with current pets.

Devon Rex

Devon Rex cats are a medium sized cat with a large head and broad chest. Devon Rex cats are a small cat breed with short coats of fur. Devon Rex cats are very energetic and often expect pets and loving attention! Devon Rex cats are jumpers who love having a perch nearby. These cats should never be brushed because their coat is fragile. However, endless hug and kisses are always welcomed.

Maine Coon

Maine Coons have been pets in the U.S. since the 1800s and is considered the oldest native cat breed. Maine Coons resemble bob cats in both their ears and their body size. Maine Coons are laid back, self-assured cats who coexist well with other pets and with young children. Despite the Maine Coon's great level of shedding, they're highly intelligent and friendly cats.

Persian

Persian cats can be traced back to the deserts of Persia and Iran. Persian cats are medium to large in size, and easily recognizable due to their flat face and long coats. Persian cats are known to find spots where they can keep an eye on their surroundings. Persians are also primarily an indoor cat and should not be let outside. Looks can be deceiving, so don't the grumpy look on a Persian cat's face deter you from adopting!
